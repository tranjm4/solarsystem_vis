Sun

The center of our Solar System.

It is a G2 V, yellow dwarf main-sequence star (G-dwarf).

Its mass is about 330,000 times that of Earth.

It is composed of primarily hydrogen (74%) and helium (24%).

Surface temperatures reach 5,500 degrees Celsius (10,000 in Fahrenheit)

Mercury

Diameter: 4,880 km

Distance from Sun: ~58 million km

Orbital period: 88 Earth days

Fun Fact: Its orbit is highly elliptical, with a perihelion that undergoes precession.

Einstein's theory of relativity explains this phenomenon.

More Information

Venus

Diameter: 12,104 km

Distance from Sun: ~108 million km

Orbital period: 225 Earth days

Fun Fact: Venus rotates in the opposite direction to most planets, and its day is longer than its year.

It also has an extremely thick atmosphere of carbon dioxide (a greenhouse gas), with surface temperatures hot enough to melt lead.

Earth

Diameter: 12,742 km

Distance from Sun: ~150 million km

Orbital period: 365.25 days

Fun Fact: Earth is the only known planet to support life, with liquid water and a breathable atmosphere.

Mars

Diameter: 6,779 km

Distance from Sun: ~228 million km

Orbital period: 687 Earth days

Fun Fact: Mars has the largest volcano in the solar system, Olympus Mons, and evidence of ancient water flows.

The Asteroid Belt

Location: Between Mars and Jupiter

Width: ~1 AU (150 million km)

Composition: Millions of rocky and metallic objects

Largest Object: Ceres (dwarf planet)

Fun Fact: The total mass of the asteroid belt is less than that of Earth's Moon.

Jupiter

Diameter: 139,820 km

Distance from Sun: ~778 million km

Orbital period: 12 Earth years

Fun Fact: Jupiter has 79 known moons and a massive storm called the Great Red Spot, which has been raging for centuries.

Winds in this storm can reach speeds upwards of 400 miles per hour.

One of its moons, Io, is the most volcanically active in the Solar System due to its close orbit and Jupiter's gravitational pull

Saturn

Diameter: 116,460 km

Distance from Sun: ~1.4 billion km

Orbital period: 29 Earth years

Fun Fact: Saturn is famous for its stunning ring system, made of ice and rock particles.

The conservation of angular momentum explains why the rings maintain their flat appearance.
